Vanderbilt University's 2026 Formula SAE team decided to reconfigure the IC car design from two external radiators on either side of the car to one larger centralized radiator. The Vanderbilt Radiator Optimization Operation for Motorsports (VROOM) Capstone team is tasked with designing a ducting system to improve cooling in this new configuration.

## Problem
<p align="center">
  <img src="/assets/img/Problem Satement.png" alt="Radiator Transition" width="400">
</p>
* Direct sufficient airflow through the radiator for engine cooling
* Keep coolant temperatures within acceptable limits (< 212°F)
* Adhere to FSAE rules and packaging constraints
* Reduce drag, weight, and polar moment of inertia

## Constraints
* Adherence to FSAE guidelines
* Packing constraints within the chassis
* No natural airflow through the internal radiator 

## Design
<p align="center">
  <img src="/assets/img/Design Diagram.png" alt="Design Diagram" width="400">
</p>
To address these constraints, a design with the following features was developed:
* Asymmetric single inlet and exhaust ducts to create a clear airpath through the system with minimal mass.
* Smooth curves, corners, and transitions to reduce turbulent pressure losses and stagnation. 
* Strategic variations in duct area and volume to utilize the Venturi effect to pull air through the radiator face, increasing overall heat transfer coefficient.
All fitting within the available space between components and chassis beams.
